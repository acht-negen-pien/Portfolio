Give the gift of Love

Critical design, reflective essay

I took a critical look at a solution for loneliness in (dementing) elderly: companion robots. These fuzzy robotic creatures imitate animals (dogs, cats, baby seals) without the inconveniences of a real animal. They have performed wonders in retirement homes. Elderly in homes are less lonely as they bond over the new ‘cat’ in the home, and advanced Alzheimer’s patients are finally smiling again. But is it ethical to give this vulnerable group of society a glorified furby to take the loneliness away? Are companion robots really a solution, or are they symptom control?
